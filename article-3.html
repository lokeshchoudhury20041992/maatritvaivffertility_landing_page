High-Risk Pregnancy: What You Need to Know

Aug 28, 2025

Dr. Prattay Ghosh

Hearing the term "high-risk pregnancy" can be concerning, but it simply means that you or your baby require extra monitoring and specialized care to ensure the best possible outcome. At Maatritva IVF, our advanced maternal care unit is dedicated to managing these pregnancies with continuous vigilance, compassion, and expertise.

What Defines a High-Risk Pregnancy?

A pregnancy may be classified as high-risk due to pre-existing maternal conditions, conditions that develop during pregnancy, or issues related to the baby.

Maternal Factors

  • Advanced Maternal Age: Being 35 years or older.
  • Chronic Health Issues: Diabetes, high blood pressure, kidney disease, or autoimmune disorders.
  • Multiple Pregnancies: Carrying twins, triplets, or more.
  • Previous Pregnancy Complications: History of preeclampsia, premature birth, or stillbirth.

Pregnancy-Related Factors

  • **Gestational Diabetes:** Diabetes that develops during pregnancy.
  • **Preeclampsia:** High blood pressure combined with signs of organ damage (usually kidneys).
  • **Placenta Previa:** The placenta partially or totally covers the mother's cervix.
  • **Fetal Issues:** Problems detected in the fetus, such as growth restriction or chromosomal abnormalities.

The Role of Advanced Maternal Monitoring

The cornerstone of managing a high-risk pregnancy is **intensive and proactive monitoring**. This goes beyond routine prenatal check-ups to allow your care team to anticipate and manage potential complications early.

Key Monitoring Techniques

At Maatritva IVF, continuous monitoring involves several specialized procedures:

  • **Level II Ultrasounds:** Detailed imaging used to check fetal growth, organ development, and the placenta's position.
  • **Non-Stress Tests (NSTs):** Monitors the baby's heart rate in response to their own movements to assess well-being.
  • **Biophysical Profile (BPP):** A non-invasive test combining ultrasound with an NST to evaluate the baby’s breathing, movement, muscle tone, amniotic fluid volume, and heart rate.
  • **Doppler Studies:** Used to measure blood flow to the placenta and baby, which is vital in cases of growth restriction.

A Coordinated Care Plan for Safety

Successful management of high-risk cases requires a **multidisciplinary team approach**. Dr. Prattay Ghosh and our specialists work closely with neonatologists, endocrinologists, and other medical experts to create a unified, personalized care plan.

  • Personalized Medication Management: Carefully adjusting medications for chronic conditions to ensure they are safe during pregnancy.
  • Strict Follow-Up Schedule: Frequent appointments and monitoring tests to catch any deviations immediately.
  • Patient Education: Empowering the mother-to-be with knowledge about warning signs and symptoms.

Ensuring Safe Outcomes

While a high-risk diagnosis sounds daunting, remember that medical advancements mean most women in this category deliver healthy babies. Our focus is on meticulous planning for both the pregnancy and the delivery. By offering advanced care and continuous support, we work tirelessly to navigate complexities and ensure a safe, positive journey for both you and your baby.

If your pregnancy has been deemed high-risk, or if you have concerns about your fertility history, contact Maatritva IVF today. We are here to provide the expert, compassionate care you deserve.
