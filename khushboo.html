Common Questions

About IVF, ICSI, infertility treatment, and gynaecology care

What is the difference between IVF and ICSI?
IVF (In Vitro Fertilisation) involves mixing eggs and sperm in a laboratory dish and allowing fertilisation to occur naturally. ICSI (Intracytoplasmic Sperm Injection) takes this further by injecting a single sperm directly into an egg — the preferred technique for male-factor infertility or where previous IVF fertilisation has failed. Dr. Khushboo recommends the most appropriate approach based on your specific diagnosis.
Who is a candidate for ICSI treatment?
ICSI is recommended for couples with severe male-factor infertility (very low sperm count, poor motility, abnormal morphology), azoospermia (no sperm in ejaculate requiring surgical retrieval), previously failed IVF fertilisation, or use of frozen/thawed eggs.
How do I know if I need IVF treatment?
IVF treatment is typically considered after simpler treatments (IUI, ovulation induction) have been unsuccessful, or when infertility is due to blocked fallopian tubes, severe endometriosis, diminished ovarian reserve, or significant male-factor issues. Dr. Khushboo Agarwal will conduct a full infertility evaluation and discuss whether IVF is the right path for you.
What gynaecological conditions does Dr. Khushboo treat?
As an experienced Gynaecologist, Dr. Khushboo Agarwal manages a wide range of conditions including PCOS (Polycystic Ovary Syndrome), endometriosis, uterine fibroids, irregular menstruation, recurrent pregnancy loss, ovarian cysts, and routine preventive womens health checks. She also provides pre-conceptional counselling to optimise your health before beginning IVF or ICSI.
What should I bring to my first infertility consultation?
Please bring any previous blood tests (AMH, FSH, LH, TSH), ultrasound reports, semen analysis reports, and previous treatment records. If you have had earlier IVF or ICSI cycles elsewhere, please bring the cycle summary and embryology reports — this helps Dr. Khushboo plan the most appropriate strategy from day one.
