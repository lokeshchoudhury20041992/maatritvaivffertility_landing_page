In standard IVF, eggs and sperm are mixed in a dish to allow fertilization to happen naturally. In ICSI, a single, healthy sperm is selected and injected directly into the egg, which is often used for male factor infertility.

A typical IVF cycle, from the start of ovarian stimulation to the embryo transfer, takes approximately 2 to 6 weeks. The timeline can vary based on the individual's treatment protocol.

Yes, egg freezing using the vitrification method is very safe. Its success depends on the woman's age at the time of freezing and the number of eggs retrieved, offering a great option for preserving future fertility.

Risks can include Ovarian Hyperstimulation Syndrome (OHSS), multiple pregnancies, and potential procedure-related complications. We minimize these risks through careful monitoring and by typically recommending a single embryo transfer (eSET). Emotional stress is also a factor, which we address with dedicated counseling and support.

Male infertility is treatable through various methods, including lifestyle changes, medications, surgery, or advanced ART procedures like ICSI, which directly address sperm-related issues.
